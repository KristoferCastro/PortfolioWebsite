ABOUT ME

Hi my name is Kristofer Castro! I am a crafty student software engineer at Simon Fraser University, a developing hobbyist artist, a technical researcher and seeker of technology, a natural problem solver at my core, a little athlete, a friendly gamer. and an all around . . . chill guy to work with!

Software Engineer

Software Engineering is an amazing art; requirements gathering, communicating with clients, designing the software, choosing technologies, programming an implementation, testing, and deployment.

Aside from my work and academic projects, I have a lot of WIP side projects like a meeting scheduler, pet feed application, and a 2D game I am working on. check out my projects

Artist

Designing the GUI of an application is one of my favorite part in software development. I enjoy using graphite pencils to create "throw-away" prototype designs and using graphic tools like Photoshop to do more finished design before implementation. glance at my art!

Researcher

Every project, academic or work, I take it on my self to make sure my group and I have the software tools and technologies we need to develop a software.

I use books, official documentations, youtube tutorials, and asking other developers to obtain knowledge.

Problem Solver

Some people like to solve crosswords, sudoku, or play some puzzle games like Angry Birds. Me, I enjoy algorithmic problems centered around finding a solution that is space and time efficient. I even have a glass white-board in my room that I use to sort out my thoughts. My never-ending goal is to improve my problem solving abilities as it is the core of software engineering.

Active

We developers probably spend an unhealthy amount of time infront of a computer. I try to keep active by playing basketball, training at my local boxing gym, go hiking, and do weekly joggings.

Gamer

One of my motives that got me studying Computing Science is my technological curiosity on how the games I played growing up were made. I currently play Dota 2.
